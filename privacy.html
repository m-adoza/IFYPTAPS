Privacy Policy

Last Updated: July 2, 2026

Introduction

IntelliVerify ("we", "our", "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our platform.

By using IntelliVerify, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this policy.

Information We Collect

We collect the following types of information:

  • Personal Identification: Full name, email address, phone number, matric number, faculty, department, and academic level.
  • Project Data: Project topics, descriptions, objectives, keywords, and supporting documents you submit.
  • Usage Data: IP address, browser type, pages visited, time and date of visits, and other diagnostic data.
  • Authentication Data: Securely hashed passwords and session tokens (managed by Supabase).

How We Use Your Information

We use the collected information for the following purposes:

  • To provide and maintain the IntelliVerify platform.
  • To process project topic submissions and plagiarism detection.
  • To facilitate communication between students, supervisors, and administrators.
  • To generate academic reports and analytics.
  • To improve system security, performance, and user experience.
  • To comply with legal and regulatory obligations.

Data Security

We implement robust security measures to protect your data, including:

  • Encrypted HTTPS communication.
  • Supabase Authentication with secure password hashing.
  • Row Level Security (RLS) to ensure data isolation between users.
  • Regular security audits and monitoring.
  • Strict access controls based on user roles.

Cookies

IntelliVerify uses session cookies to maintain your authenticated session. These cookies are essential for the platform to function correctly and do not store sensitive personal information.

Data Sharing

We do not sell, trade, or rent your personal information to third parties. Data may be shared with:

  • Your assigned supervisor and administrators for academic purposes.
  • University officials as required for academic administration.
  • Legal authorities if required by law.

Your Rights

You have the following rights regarding your data:

  • Access your personal information.
  • Update or correct your profile details.
  • Request deletion of your account (subject to academic record retention policies).
  • Opt-out of non-essential communications.

Data Retention

We retain your data for as long as necessary to provide our services and comply with academic record-keeping requirements. Project topics and plagiarism reports may be retained for academic integrity and audit purposes.
